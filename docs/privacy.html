Privacy Policy

Dream Day · Last updated: July 2026

Dream Day is a daily planner and journal designed so that your personal data stays personal. This policy explains, in plain language, exactly what happens with your information.

Your data lives on your device

Your schedule, journal notes, pillars, streaks, and progress are stored locally on your phone. Dream Day has no accounts, no logins, and no server-side database of user data. We cannot read your journal, and if you delete the app, that data is deleted with it. You can create your own backups from the Plan tab; those backup files are yours and are never sent to us.

AI Coach features

When you use an AI-powered feature (personalized activity suggestions or spoken schedule parsing), the app sends only the minimum text needed for that request — for example, recent activity titles and short journal snippets for the relevant pillar, or the schedule text you dictated — to our server, which forwards it to Anthropic's Claude API for processing and returns the result. These requests are not linked to your name or identity, are used only to generate your response, and are not used to build profiles or train models by us. See Anthropic's privacy policy for how the API processor handles requests. If you never use AI features, no journal content leaves your device.

Dictation

Voice input uses your device's speech recognition. Microphone access is requested only when you tap the mic button, and audio is used solely to produce the text you see on screen.

Subscriptions

Coach Premium is purchased through Apple. Payment is handled entirely by Apple; we never see your payment details. We use RevenueCat, a subscription-management service, to verify active subscriptions using an anonymous identifier. Manage or cancel anytime in your device Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.

What we don't do

No ads. No selling of data. No tracking across other apps or websites. No analytics that identify you personally.

Children

Dream Day is intended for users 13 and older and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.
